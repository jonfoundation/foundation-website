How to Plan a Home Renovation

A step-by-step guide to planning your renovation with confidence, avoiding costly mistakes, and making informed decisions before, during, and after construction.

By Jon Lourash · Foundation Renovation Coaching

Planning Is the Most Important Part of Any Renovation

When most people imagine renovating their home, they picture the exciting parts—not the planning. After more than a decade managing renovation projects, I've found the projects with the happiest homeowners almost always begin with a clear plan.

Planning isn't the most exciting part of a renovation, but it is the foundation everything else is built on.

Already Started Your Renovation?

This guide isn't just for homeowners who are starting from scratch. Whether you're comparing bids, working with a designer, or already under construction, Foundation Renovation Coaching is designed to meet you wherever you are in the process.

The Foundation Roadmap™

Discover · Plan · Design · Prepare · Build · Complete

A successful renovation is not one decision. It is a sequence of decisions, and each phase affects the next. The roadmap below helps organize those decisions in a practical order.

01

Discover

Define your goals before requesting bids or choosing finishes. Ask why you're renovating, what problems you're solving, how long you plan to stay, and what success looks like.

Questions to consider

  • What is not working in your home today?
  • Which outcomes are essential, and which would simply be nice to have?
  • How long do you expect to remain in the home?
  • What would make the finished renovation feel successful?
02

Plan

Build a budget that includes more than construction costs. Consider design, engineering, permits, furnishings, temporary housing, storage, financing, landscaping, and contingency.

Think Beyond Construction Costs

Many renovation decisions involve tradeoffs. For example, living in your home during construction may save temporary housing costs, but it can also increase disruption, extend the schedule, or reduce construction efficiency.

There isn't one correct answer—the right decision depends on your priorities. Understanding the tradeoffs is the key.

Prioritize Your Wish List

Separate your ideas into Must Have, Nice to Have, and Future Phase categories. This gives you a framework for making decisions if the scope, budget, or schedule needs to change.

03

Design

Choose the team that's right for your project. Architects, designers, design-build firms, and contractors all bring different strengths.

The best structure depends on your project's complexity, the level of design support you want, how much responsibility you want to retain, and how you prefer to make decisions. Focus on understanding the options rather than assuming one approach is always best.

04

Prepare

Choose the right contractor, understand proposals and exclusions, verify insurance and licensing, and build a realistic schedule that accounts for permits and lead times.

Before signing, make sure you understand what is included, what is excluded, how allowances work, how changes will be priced, and what responsibilities remain with you.

05

Build

Construction is really about communication. Ask questions, understand change orders, document decisions, and stay focused on your goals.

You do not need to manage the contractor's work, but you should understand the information being presented to you and keep a clear record of major decisions, approvals, and changes.

06

Complete

Finish with a walkthrough, punch list, warranties, maintenance information, and closeout documentation.

Before final payment, confirm incomplete items are documented and that you have the information needed to operate and maintain the finished work.

Final Thoughts

The more time you spend planning—and the more informed your decisions are throughout the process—the better your renovation experience will be. Foundation Renovation Coaching exists to help homeowners make confident renovation decisions before, during, and after construction.
